# Equipment Authorization and Delivery Policy

## Policy Number: EQP-001
## Department: Operations / Patient Services
## Effective Date: January 1, 2026
## Last Reviewed: March 1, 2026

## Purpose
To standardize the equipment authorization, ordering, delivery, and setup process across all Home Health locations to ensure timely patient access to prescribed DME while maintaining compliance with payer requirements.

## Prior Authorization Requirements by Payer

### Medicare Part B
- Oxygen equipment (E0431-E0444): No prior authorization required, but CMN must be on file
- CPAP/BiPAP (E0601, E0470, E0471): No prior auth, but sleep study and face-to-face required
- Power wheelchairs (K0856-K0864): Prior Authorization Required (PAR) since 2023
- Ventilators (E0465): Prior authorization recommended for high-cost items

### Medicare Advantage Plans
- Most require prior authorization for all DME over $500 value
- Typical approval timeline: 3-5 business days for standard, 24-72 hours for urgent
- Peer-to-peer review available for denied pre-authorizations

### Commercial Payers (UHC, Aetna, BCBS)
- Prior auth generally required for: All respiratory equipment, power mobility, hospital beds
- Prior auth NOT required for: Canes, walkers (standard), supplies/accessories
- Submit via payer portal or fax with supporting clinical documentation

## Delivery and Setup Standards

### Timeline Requirements
- Standard equipment: Delivery within 48-72 hours of authorization
- Urgent equipment (hospital discharge): Same-day or next-day delivery
- Complex equipment (ventilators, power chairs): Within 5-7 business days
- Supply reorders: Ship within 24 hours via standard shipping

### Patient Setup Checklist
1. Verify patient identity (two identifiers: name and DOB)
2. Deliver equipment to prescribed location (home, ALF, group home)
3. Inspect equipment for damage and proper function
4. Train patient/caregiver on proper use:
   - Operation of controls and settings
   - Cleaning and maintenance requirements
   - Safety precautions and emergency procedures
   - When to contact Home Health for issues
5. Document training completion with patient signature
6. Verify equipment serial number matches order
7. Confirm delivery date and time for billing accuracy
8. Schedule follow-up call for 72 hours post-delivery

### Equipment Categories and Rental/Purchase Rules
- Capped Rental (most DME): 13-month rental period, then ownership transfers to patient
- Oxygen Equipment: 36-month rental period, supplier maintains ownership
- Supplies: Purchased items (masks, tubing, filters)
- Inexpensive/Routinely Purchased: Items under $150 (patient choice: rent or buy)

## Compliance Documentation
All deliveries must have:
- Signed Delivery Ticket with patient/caregiver signature
- Equipment serial number recorded in system
- Training completion acknowledgment
- Advance Beneficiary Notice (ABN) if coverage uncertain
- Photo documentation for power mobility equipment
