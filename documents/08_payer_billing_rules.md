# Payer-Specific Billing Rules and Requirements

## Policy Number: PAY-001
## Department: Revenue Cycle Management
## Effective Date: January 1, 2026
## Last Reviewed: February 1, 2026

## Purpose
To document payer-specific billing rules that differ from standard CMS guidelines, reducing administrative denials caused by payer variation.

## Medicare Part B (Traditional)

### Key Rules
- Fiscal Intermediary/MAC: Varies by region (Noridian, CGS, Palmetto GBA, NGS)
- Timely filing: 365 days from date of service
- CMN requirement: Mandatory for oxygen and qualifying respiratory equipment
- Same/similar equipment rule: Cannot bill for same type if patient has existing equipment from another supplier (check eligibility first)
- Competitive Bidding: Certain DME categories subject to DMEPOS Competitive Bidding Program pricing
- AB modifier requirement: For items in competitive bid areas to beneficiaries outside the area

### Common Medicare Denial Codes
- CO-4: Procedure code inconsistent with modifier (often RR vs NU mismatch)
- CO-16: Information missing (CMN, physician signature, test results)
- CO-27: Expenses incurred after coverage terminated
- CO-96: Non-covered charge (item does not meet LCD criteria)
- CO-197: Prior auth/precertification absent (power mobility devices)
- N362: Missing/incomplete signed CMN

### LCD/NCD References
- LCD L33797: Oxygen and Oxygen Equipment
- LCD L33718: CPAP/BiPAP devices
- LCD L33788: Power Mobility Devices
- LCD L33686: Hospital Beds

## Medicare Advantage (MA) Plans

### Key Differences from Traditional Medicare
- Each MA plan can impose additional requirements beyond original Medicare
- Prior authorization more frequently required
- Network restrictions may apply (ensure Home Health is in-network for specific plan)
- May require different forms than standard CMS CMN
- Appeals go to plan first, then to CMS Independent Review Entity (IRE)
- Some plans require letter of medical necessity INSTEAD of CMN

### Top MA Payers for Home Health
- UnitedHealthcare Medicare Advantage: 35% of MA volume
- Humana Medicare Advantage: 25% of MA volume
- Aetna Medicare Advantage: 15% of MA volume
- BCBS Medicare Advantage: 12% of MA volume
- Other regional plans: 13%

## Medicaid (State-Specific)

### General Medicaid Rules
- Prior authorization almost always required for DME
- Reimbursement rates typically 60-80% of Medicare fee schedule
- Some states require separate DME license/enrollment
- Timely filing limits shorter than Medicare (90 days in many states)
- Dual-eligible patients (Medicare + Medicaid): Medicare primary, Medicaid secondary for copay/deductible

### High-Volume State Rules
- Florida: Electronic PA required via Availity, 90-day timely filing
- Texas: PA through TMHP portal, 95-day timely filing
- California: Medi-Cal requires Treatment Authorization Request (TAR), 180-day filing
- New York: ePACES system for PA, 90-day timely filing
- Pennsylvania: PROMISe system, 180-day timely filing

## Commercial Payers

### UnitedHealthcare (UHC)
- PA required for all DME >$500
- Submit via UHC Provider Portal or fax to (800) xxx-xxxx
- Standard PA decision: 5 business days
- Urgent PA decision: 24-72 hours
- Timely filing: 180 days from DOS
- Appeal deadline: 180 days from denial date

### Aetna
- PA via Availity or NaviNet
- Aetna precertification list updated quarterly
- Timely filing: 180 days from DOS
- Unique requirement: Functional Capacity Form for mobility equipment
- Appeal deadline: 60 days from denial

### Blue Cross Blue Shield (BCBS)
- Varies significantly by state plan (36 independent companies)
- Most require PA for DME
- Many use Evicore for PA management
- Timely filing: Varies by plan (90-365 days)
- Key tip: Always verify specific plan rules — BCBS is NOT one payer

## Electronic Submission Standards
- All payers: Submit via ANSI X12 837P (Professional) electronic format
- Clearinghouse partners: Change Healthcare, Availity, Trizetto
- Target electronic submission rate: 98%+ (paper claims only when required)
- Acknowledgment monitoring: Check 999/277CA responses within 24 hours
