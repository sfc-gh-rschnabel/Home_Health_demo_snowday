# Referral Management and Physician Onboarding Guidelines

## Policy Number: REF-001
## Department: Sales / Patient Intake
## Effective Date: January 1, 2026
## Last Reviewed: February 28, 2026

## Purpose
To standardize the referral intake process, physician onboarding workflow, and ongoing referral source management to maximize conversion rates and minimize referral leakage.

## Referral Sources

### Primary Referral Channels
1. Hospital Discharge Planning (30% of volume): Patients being discharged who need home DME
2. Physician Office Referrals (35%): Established patients needing respiratory/mobility equipment
3. Sleep Lab Referrals (15%): Patients diagnosed with OSA needing CPAP/BiPAP
4. Pulmonary Rehabilitation (10%): Post-rehab patients needing ongoing respiratory support
5. Home Health Agency Referrals (10%): Coordinated care patients needing DME alongside nursing

### Referral Priority Levels
- STAT (within 4 hours): Hospital discharge with immediate equipment need (oxygen, ventilator)
- Urgent (within 24 hours): Post-discharge next-day setup, escalated physician request
- Routine (within 48-72 hours): Standard new patient setup
- Scheduled (specific date): Pre-planned equipment delivery (surgery, sleep study follow-up)

## New Physician Onboarding Process

### Step 1: Qualification (Day 1-5)
- Verify physician NPI is active and enrolled in PECOS (for Medicare referrals)
- Confirm specialty aligns with Lincare's equipment portfolio
- Check geographic proximity to Lincare service area
- Research current DME supplier relationship (competitive intelligence)
- Assign to territory rep based on location

### Step 2: Initial Contact (Day 5-10)
- Sales rep introduces Lincare capabilities and service differentiators
- Provide welcome packet: catalog, referral forms, contact information
- Identify key office contact (office manager, medical assistant, referral coordinator)
- Offer lunch-and-learn or in-service presentation

### Step 3: First Referral (Day 10-30)
- Process first referral with white-glove service
- Expedite delivery and setup (same-day or next-day regardless of standard timeline)
- Sales rep follows up with physician office within 48 hours of delivery
- Document any issues or feedback for immediate resolution

### Step 4: Relationship Building (Day 30-90)
- Monthly face-to-face visit by sales rep
- Quarterly business review: referral volume, patient outcomes, service metrics
- Identify additional equipment categories physician could refer for
- Address any billing or authorization issues proactively

## Referral Processing Workflow

### Incoming Referral
1. Referral received (fax, phone, EHR interface, or web portal)
2. Intake coordinator verifies: patient eligibility, physician order completeness, insurance coverage
3. Prior authorization initiated if required by payer
4. Equipment ordered/allocated from local inventory
5. Delivery scheduled with patient
6. Confirmation sent to referring physician
7. Post-delivery follow-up documented

### Referral Completion Targets
- Eligibility verification: Within 2 hours of referral receipt
- Authorization initiation: Same business day
- Patient contact for scheduling: Within 4 hours
- Equipment delivery: Per priority level above
- Physician confirmation: Within 24 hours of delivery
- Compliance documentation complete: Within 48 hours

## Referral Leakage Prevention
Referral leakage occurs when a physician intends to refer to Lincare but the referral is lost. Common causes and prevention:
- Incomplete referral form → Dedicated intake team calls physician office within 2 hours
- Authorization delays → Proactive payer follow-up; escalation at 48 hours
- Patient non-response → 3-attempt contact protocol (phone, then text, then mail)
- Inventory shortage → Cross-location inventory check; expedited shipping from distribution center
- Competitive intercept → Rapid response within 24 hours of referral receipt

## Performance Metrics
- Referral-to-setup conversion rate: Target 85%+
- Time from referral to first contact: Target < 4 hours
- Time from referral to delivery: Target < 72 hours (routine)
- Physician satisfaction (survey): Target 4.5/5.0
- Referral leakage rate: Target < 8%
