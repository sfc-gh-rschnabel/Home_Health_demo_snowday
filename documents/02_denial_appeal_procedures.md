# Home Health Denial Appeal Procedures

## Policy Number: DEN-001
## Department: Revenue Cycle Management - Appeals Unit
## Effective Date: January 1, 2026
## Last Reviewed: February 28, 2026

## Purpose
To establish a standardized process for appealing denied claims, maximizing recovery rates, and ensuring all appealable denials are addressed within payer-required timeframes.

## Appeal Timelines by Payer
- Medicare Part B: 120 days from date on Remittance Advice (RA) for redetermination
- Medicare Advantage: 60 days from denial notice for standard reconsideration
- Medicaid: Varies by state (30-90 days typically)
- UnitedHealthcare: 180 days from denial date
- Aetna: 180 days from date of service or 60 days from denial (whichever is later)
- BCBS: Per plan-specific terms, typically 90-180 days

## Appeal Levels

### Level 1: Redetermination (Medicare) / Reconsideration (Commercial)
- Filed with the original payer/MAC
- Must include cover letter stating reason for appeal
- Attach all supporting documentation (CMN, medical records, prescriptions)
- Expected turnaround: 30-60 days
- Success rate target: 40% overturn rate

### Level 2: Reconsideration by QIC (Medicare) / Formal Appeal (Commercial)
- Filed with Qualified Independent Contractor (Medicare) or payer appeals dept
- Must include additional evidence not previously submitted
- Expected turnaround: 60-90 days
- Success rate target: 25% overturn rate

### Level 3: Administrative Law Judge (Medicare) / External Review
- Amount in controversy must meet threshold ($180 for 2026)
- Hearing requested within 60 days of Level 2 decision
- May include expert testimony or peer-to-peer review
- Success rate target: 50%+ at ALJ level

## Root Cause Response Matrix

### Documentation Denials (CO-16, CO-236, CO-11)
Required response:
- Obtain missing CMN or updated medical records from physician
- Submit corrected documentation with appeal letter referencing original claim
- Include timeline showing reasonable effort to obtain documentation
- Priority: HIGH - These are most frequently overturned

### Authorization Denials (CO-197, CO-252)
Required response:
- Request retroactive authorization from payer
- Submit evidence that authorization was requested prior to service
- Include urgency/emergency circumstances if applicable
- Escalate to physician for peer-to-peer review if available

### Coverage Denials (CO-27, CO-50, CO-96)
Required response:
- Review payer contract and LCD/NCD for specific coverage criteria
- Submit medical necessity documentation proving patient meets criteria
- Reference specific LCD/NCD articles supporting coverage
- Consider requesting Medical Director review

### Technical Denials (CO-4, CO-18, CO-29)
Required response:
- CO-4 (modifier issue): Correct and resubmit as corrected claim
- CO-18 (duplicate): Verify not truly duplicate; submit with explanation
- CO-29 (timely filing): Submit proof of original timely submission

## Escalation Procedures
1. Day 0-5: Denial received, categorized, assigned to specialist
2. Day 5-10: Research completed, supporting documents gathered
3. Day 10-15: Appeal drafted and submitted to payer
4. Day 45: Follow up if no response received
5. Day 60: Escalate to supervisor if still pending
6. Day 90: Consider Level 2 appeal if Level 1 unsuccessful

## Performance Metrics
- Appeal submission rate: Target 90% of eligible denials appealed
- First-level overturn rate: Target 40%
- Average turnaround: Target < 15 days from denial to appeal submission
- Recovery rate: Target 60%+ on appealed denials
- Revenue recovered per specialist FTE: Track monthly for productivity benchmarking
