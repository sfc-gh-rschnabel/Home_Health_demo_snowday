# Medicare Certificate of Medical Necessity (CMN) Requirements

## Policy Number: CMN-001
## Department: Clinical Documentation / Intake
## Effective Date: January 1, 2026
## Last Reviewed: March 1, 2026

## Purpose
To ensure all Certificates of Medical Necessity are completed accurately and completely before claim submission, reducing CMN-related denials which represent approximately 25% of all Lincare Medicare denials.

## CMN Form Types

### CMS-484: Oxygen Equipment
Required for: E0431, E0433, E0424, E0434, E0439, E0441, E0442, E0443, E0444
Key Requirements:
- Section A: Patient demographics (must match Medicare records exactly)
- Section B: Completed by supplier with equipment details, HCPCS codes, rental/purchase status
- Section C: Physician narrative explaining medical necessity
- Section D: Physician signature and date (signature date cannot precede testing date)

Critical Clinical Data:
- Qualifying blood gas study (ABG or pulse oximetry)
- Testing must be performed while patient is on room air or prescribed liter flow
- Results must show: PaO2 <= 55 mmHg OR SaO2 <= 88%
- Alternative qualification: PaO2 56-59 mmHg with dependent edema, pulmonary hypertension, or erythrocytosis
- Testing must be performed within 30 days prior to initial certification
- Recertification required every 12 months (within 90 days before or after anniversary)

Common CMN-484 Errors Leading to Denials:
1. Blood gas values not meeting threshold requirements
2. Testing performed while patient on supplemental oxygen (must be room air for initial)
3. Physician signature dated before test date
4. Missing liter flow prescription
5. Blank fields in Section C (all questions must be answered)
6. CMN older than 90 days at time of claim submission

### CMS-10125: CPAP/BiPAP Equipment
Required for: E0601 (CPAP), E0470 (BiPAP without backup), E0471 (BiPAP with backup)
Key Requirements:
- Qualifying sleep study showing AHI >= 5 events per hour
- Face-to-face clinical evaluation by treating physician within 90 days prior to initial setup
- For CPAP: Diagnosis of OSA (G47.33) confirmed by sleep study
- For BiPAP: Documentation of failed CPAP trial (minimum 3-month usage attempt) OR severe OSA/CSA criteria met

CPAP Compliance Requirements:
- Initial period: Patient must use device >= 4 hours per night on 70% of nights during consecutive 30-day period within first 90 days
- Compliance data must be downloaded and documented between day 31 and day 91
- If non-compliant, authorization for continued rental is denied
- Physician must document continued medical necessity if compliance criteria not met

### DME Information Form (DIF)
Required for: Wheelchairs (K0001-K0004), hospital beds (E0260-E0266)
Key Requirements:
- Face-to-face examination by physician within 45 days prior to order
- Mobility limitation documented with functional assessment
- Home assessment for wheelchair accessibility and bed placement
- For power wheelchairs: Specialty exam by PT, OT, or physician with mobility expertise

## CMN Processing Workflow
1. Intake coordinator identifies CMN requirement based on HCPCS code
2. Blank CMN pre-populated with patient demographics (Section A) and equipment details (Section B)
3. CMN sent to physician for Section C completion and signature
4. Physician returns completed CMN within 30 days (follow up at day 7, 14, 21)
5. Quality check: Verify all fields complete, dates logical, test results qualifying
6. Scan and attach to patient file and claim record
7. Submit claim with CMN reference number

## Physician Education Initiative
Lincare provides CMN completion training to referring physicians to reduce rejection rates:
- Initial onboarding packet with common completion errors
- Quarterly newsletter highlighting top denial reasons
- Dedicated CMN support line: 1-800-xxx-xxxx option 4
- On-site training available for high-volume practices (20+ referrals/month)
